Satoru Iwata

Granddaddy of Nintendo

Satoru Iwata holding the 3DS

A timeline of Satoru Iwata's lifetime:

  • 1959 - Satoru Iwata is born in Hokkaido Prefecture.
  • 1978 - 1982 - Iwata studies at the Tokyo Institute of Technology University.
  • 1983 - Iwata joins HAL Laboratory, as a designer, engineer, programmer, and marketer. He goes on to work on developing the Earthbound, Kirby, and Super Smash Bros. franchises.
  • 1993 - Iwata becomes president of HAL Laboratories, steering it from financial jeopardy to solvency.
  • 1999 - The first Super Smash Bros. game is released, directed by Iwata and Masahiro Sakurai.
  • 2000 - Iwata joins Nintendo, overseeing the Coporate Planning Division.
  • 2002 - Iwata is named as President of Nintendo, being the first president outside of the founding Yamauchi family, and only the fourth since the company was founded in 1889.
  • 2004 - The Nintendo DS is released, the first console under Iwata's presidency.
  • 2006 - Iwata begins an ongoing series of interviews with Nintendo game makers and hardware designers, known as Iwata Asks. He uses this platform to announce the Nintendo Wii, released later the same year. This console targeted non-gamers, a key aspect of Iwata's business philosophy.
  • 2011 - The Nintendo 3DS is released. Iwata reaches out directly to his fans through Nintendo Direct presentation, personally presenting information about Nintendo releases.
  • 2012 - The Nintendo Wii U is released.
  • 2014 - After a low year of Nintendo profits, Iwata announces that he will halve his own pay, among other executive pay cuts, to show his dedication for the company. Later the same year, he misses the Electronic Entertainment Expo due to health reasons.
  • 2015 - On July 11, Satoru Iwata passes away at age 55.
